Error Reports and Warnings

Error Reports

The Level Editor will check for the following possible sources of errors and a set of necessary conditions before the file is saved or exported. If one of these errors occurs, you'll get an error message and the Level Editor will refuse to save the map as a .map file and you won't be able to export it as a Game Map file.

  • There must be at least one island.
    This simply means that you haven't put any terrain at all on your map.
  • The map cannot contain more than 255 islands.
    Generally, there can be two reasons for this. You could have too many islands. Please note that the Level Editor defines islands as any unit of land, however small, which is surrounded by water. It also defines islands as any mass of rocky terrain, no matter how small, which is surrounded by snow. This error occurs most frequently when you use the spray or sprinkle functions for placing terrain and snow in your map.
  • Player 1 has to be a human player.
    Please check this and correct it, if necessary.
  • Mixed Setups aren't allowed -- mixed Setups are those where default setups are used for some players and manual setups are used for other players. All players must have either default settings for settlers and buildings, or all parties must have individual settings for settlers and buildings.
    When you have placed a settler, a building or a pile of goods on your map, you must do this for every player because the Level Editor can then no longer place default values for the remaining parties.
  • When setting victory conditions under "Annihilate Players", you cannot check your own player or an undefined player.
    Please check this and correct it, if necessary.
  • When setting victory conditions under "Destroy Buildings", you cannot check your own player or and undefined player.
    Please check this and correct it, if necessary.
  • When setting victory conditions under "Time", you can check only your own players.
    Please check this and correct it, if necessary.
  • When setting victory conditions under "Locations", you can only enter locations that lie outside your own starting territory.
    Please check this and correct it, if necessary.
  • Starting positions shouldn't be placed in water, on snow, rocky terrain, swamps or outside the player's territory.
  • Starting positions must fulfill the following conditions.

If the player already has a building:

  • The terrain for the starting position must be grass. For the Egyptians, it may also be desert.
  • The terrain on which the starting position lies must be free of buildings and objects.
  • The terrain on which the starting position lies must belong to the corresponding player.

If the player doesn't yet have a building:

  • approx. a 70-field circle with the starting point as center must be grass. For the Egyptians, it can also be desert.
  • approx. a 70-field circle with the starting point as center may not belong to another player.
  • approx. a 70-field circle with the starting point as center may not contain objects or buildings.
  • All starting positions require at least a 50-field radius from one another.

When the player doesn't yet have a building, it is also important that the starting point isn't located in an area with extreme increases in elevation, since the starting tower can't be placed there. This doesn't produce an error message.

Please check this and correct it, if necessary.

  • Neither computer opponents nor "selectable" opponents, the ones marked as "Choose" in the "Played by" box in the Player Settings Window, can be placed on separated areas of your map.
    Players without human intelligence require an area which is not separated because the Level Editor cannot recognize why carriers with stone cannot get to a construction site in a separated area.

 

Warning Reports

Warning messages are displayed when the Level Editor notices that some of the settings that you have chosen could cause problems. The warning message doesn't necessarily mean that the map is unplayable.

  • You haven't entered any description text for your map. (Only for single player maps)
    You are not required to provide a description for your map but the Level Editor assumes that when no text is present, that it is an oversight and not your intention to leave players in the dark about what expects them in your map.
  • You haven't entered any text for Tips and Tricks. (Only for single player maps)
    Do the players of your map a favor and give them some helpful instructions.
  • There are no trees on the map; your woodcutter has nothing to do.
    Provided that you've placed enough wood on the map so that players can at least build a forester's hut, a woodcutter's hut and a sawmill, the map is playable. No trees make for a very barren landscape, though.
  • There is no building stone on the map; your stonecutter has nothing to do.
    If you have hidden piles of building stone throughout the map, it will be playable. If you haven't done this, players won't be able to build anything, not a single building.
  • There are no resources on the map; your miners have nothing to do and your fisherman has no fish to catch.
    This map is playable if you have hidden piles of resources throughout the map. Players have no reason to build any mines or any of the food production buildings for feeding the miners. This could easily become a very boring game.
  • There is no computer opponent. (Only for single player games)
    If your map is only intended for peaceful settling, a computer opponent is not necessary. Players shouldn't expect any epic battles from this map.
  • No victory conditions have been entered; the map can be played but not won. (Only for single player maps)
    This map can be played forever, but players cannot look forward to a message telling them that they have won.

   This warning message is also displayed when "Enable Victory Condition" is on but no concrete conditions have been entered.

  • There are fewer Settlers / Goods on your map than recommended.
    These warning messages are displayed only after at least one building has been placed on the map for at least one player. This doesn't necessarily mean the map is not playable. You should consider whether you have a good reason for deviating from the minimal recommended levels.

 A couple short explanations follow concerning what can happen when not enough tools or Settlers are available.

 

Hammers/Smelters/Builders

This warning appears when you have placed fewer than the recommended number of hammers, smelters or builders. Smelters and builders need hammers. If you have too few of them, there can be no new builders or smelters. This could lead to a bottleneck in later phases of weapon production or even in the first building projects.

Please also keep in mind that, after you've built a residence, a certain percentage of the new Settlers will become builders and that they need hammers. If you don't have a smelter by then, your game could easily lead to a dead end. If you don't need any further builders, you can adjust this in the game.

 

Scythes/Farmers

Place at least one scythe on the map because players will need food for their miners. Without mines and in turn without metal, the tool smith cannot produce scythes.

 

Picks/Miners

If you have too few picks, they may all be alloted to the stonecutters before you have any miners. Without picks the player won't have any miners for his mines. Make sure you have enough picks and at least two miners.

 

Swordsmen

If you have too few swordsmen, new guard towers cannot be garrisoned and the expansion of the colony will draw to a halt..

 

Carriers

If you have too few carriers, your economy slows to a snail's pace. The more carriers you have, the faster goods can be distributed.

 

Shovels/Diggers

If too few shovels and/or diggers are available, construction takes considerably longer. Without any shovel and diggers, the settlers won't be able to build unless you already have a tool smith who can produce shovels.

 

Wood/Stone

So that you can get started building without delay, you need boards and stones from the very beginning. You could, of course, want a delay for strategic reasons, but make sure that building is possible.

 

Woodcutters/Sawmill Workers/Stonecutters

To avoid delays, you need production capacity for building materials. If you intend to have delays, be sure that construction is possible.
