Tips for Quick Starters

Here is information for those of you who want to jump right in making your own maps and levels. Working with the Level Editor and trying out the different functions is sometimes the best way to learn. There are a couple things to watch out for though:

  • You can only set the size of a map once, and that is when you create it. Subsequent changes to the size are no longer possible.
  • Under "Map Settings" in the "Edit" menu, you choose whether you want to create a single or multiplayer map. This setting can be changed later.
  • You can view a map that you are currently working on within the game without any problems. To do so, click on "View Map in Game" in the "File" menu.
  • To view a map in the game, you have to save it first. If it is a multiplayer map, you should name your map so that it contains the numbers of players it is designed for. "5x2P_HC.edm", for example, would indicate that the map is designed for five parties, each consisting one human player and one computer player. This serves as a helpful orientation for potential players. No limits are set to your imagination with regard to naming single player maps.
  • When you edit buildings or Settlers, it is very important that you have the correct player activated for the buildings or settlers you want to edit. The player can be selected at the bottom of the "Tool Setup Window".
  • As soon as you've placed just one guard tower on your map, you have to do this for all players because the Level Editor will then no longer assign the initial goods piles.
  • Buildings can only be placed on territory that the active player controls. As in the game, guard towers and castles determine control of the territory.
  • The Level Editor distinguishes between these different types of information: terrain, elevation, buildings, settlers, landscape objects, resources and goods piles.
  • You cannot put all objects, buildings, and Settlers on every kind of terrain. You cannot, for example, put cactuses in snow, or build a fisherman's hut in the desert.
  • When you select an object, a building, a Settler or goods pile, the appropriate symbol for what you've selected will attach itself to the mouse pointer. When this symbol is grayed-out, it is not possible to set your object in the area where your mouse is currently located. When the symbol is displayed in color, the object can be placed in this area of the map.
  • You should raise or lower the terrain on your map before you place any objects on it.
  • Don't forget to put rivers on your map!
  • In order that your map is playable, you must export it using "Export Game Map File..." in the "File" menu.
    •

    When you export the map, you may be warned of errors. You can find more information about this in the section, "Error Reports and Warnings".
