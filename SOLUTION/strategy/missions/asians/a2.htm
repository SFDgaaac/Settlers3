CIVIL WAR

VICTORY CONDITION: Destroy the Asian settlement in the Southeast!

DEFEAT CONDITION: You lose all your buildings.

SITUATION: The Egyptian colony in the East (Ra) is well defended. You'll need to concentrate on the Asian settlement in the Southeast (A). They can become a menace if you expand too slowly. Speed is of the essence!

2. DIARY ENTRY OF TSU TANG:

nited, we are invincible. The pirates have found rich deposits of gold on an island in the South, and now arm themselves against us. And they are supported with food by the treacherous Egyptians, led by Ramadamses. He has proven adept at setting us against each other, so he must be reminded who he opposes.

Regrettably, the Egyptian settlement is too well developed for any attack to meet with success, but I wonder if he is ready to wage war over his small band of lice-ridden pirates?"

 

Solution from:

 Tobias

 

n this mission you will often be attacked at the isthmus by the Egyptians. You won’t have to worry about any attacks from the south. By building a castle and large tower, and manning them with bowmen, you’ll be able to restore peace. You can then attack the your opponent in the east via your opponent in the south with an army of about 20 soldiers. You can even attack from two sides…

 

Solution from:

 Marcel Jeske

 

ou can ensure a quick start by building four woodcutter’s huts, a medium residence, and then two sawmills. You’ll only need a stonecutter’s hut later. You should expand to the west mainly and secure the weapons you’ll find lying around there.

It’s important to begin producing gold quickly. With a fighting strength of 60% you can already overrun your blue opponent without any difficulties. You should ignore the Egyptians since they are not important for fulfilling the goals of the mission..

 

Solution from:

 Alfred Teichmann

 

he brutal (ly easy) way: this solution is mostly the result of coincidence and curiosity.

I sent out the soldiers I had to look for the enemy, first to the south and then to the east. My first wary attempt at approaching the closest tower was immediately successful. The poor Egyptians, totally unprepared, must have assumed that their opponents were probably still far off. One tower after the other fell and then came the surprising message that I had won the game. In short, no new buildings constructed, no promotion of soldiers and yet the game won (Settlers Blitzkrieg?). To be recommended: set the lowest resolution with F1 (the individual soldiers can be controlled better with the mouse) and pull your injured soldiers out of battle!
