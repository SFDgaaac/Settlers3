DIVIDE AND CONQUER

VICTORY CONDITION: Conquer the entire island!

DEFEAT CONDITION: You lose all your buildings.

SITUATION: Ramadamses (Ra1) has his settlement spread from North to South. His southern flank is well prepared and continues to grow strong. At the moment, you are too weak to attack him from the South. Prepare to mount a well-directed attack against his weakly developed northern flank instead (Ra2).

4. DIARY ENTRY OF TSU TANG:

o the East our back is still not free, but Ramadamses follows too closely on my heels. For that we will now strike at the heart of his lands and repel him. He may then forge his futile plans of revenge in the distance. 

For my advance in the South, I will need more strength on my flanks, but even now Ramadamses moves in that direction. I must cut him off and divert his strength. Perhaps a feigned attack in the North is the answer?

 

Solution from:

 Clemens Adolphs

 

ou have to begin mining in the northern mountains immediately so that you can recruit as many soldiers as possible. First, you should build a sawmill and then a small or medium residence would be helpful.

The two land bridges that separate your territory from enemy territory should each be secured with a large tower. Once you have a half decent army together (15-20 swordsmen, at least 5 bowmen), you should capture the small tower that is near the outlook tower.

Send all of the soldiers you continue to recruit there. If you want, you can try to capture the area where the two large outlook towers to the east are located. This will deprive your opponent of sources for important raw materials.

Now it is time to expand to the south. Take steps to build up your production of sulfur because your opponent has a castle that he keeps fully garrisoned. It is also necessary to build up your construction industry near the mountain. This will speed up development considerably and you will also free up a lot of space. Don’t forget your production of manna!

Once you have one or two fully loaded cannons with a protective escort of bowmen and spearmen, you should attack and destroy the castle. The castle is located across the land bridge to the east of the southern mountain. Your opponent has a huge supply of gems there that you should capture as quickly as possible. The rest is quite simple: Recruit and promote soldiers. Now you can literally overrun Ramadamses.

 

Solution from:

 Marcel Jeske

 

here are a lot of trees surrounding the starting position. You can make good use of this abundance of trees with four woodcutters. You could also use a sawmill and some more settlers. Then nothing should stop you from building up quickly.

In the northwest and in the south there are enough mineral resources available. In the south there is also enough room for farms and other food production (after deforesting the area). The Egyptians will begin disturbing your building plans and attack mostly in the far north. The fewer opportunities your opponent has for attacking, the better his attacks can be repelled. For this reason, big towers and castles are not so important. Towers near the border, however, are of critical importance. 

As soon as you have enough new recruits and your supply of gold is in order, you can begin to take the offensive role. It is good to be aware that your opponent has his gold storage in the southeast: this can be more useful for you than for him.
