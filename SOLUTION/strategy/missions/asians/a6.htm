FERTILE NEW LANDS

VICTORY CONDITION: Destroy your enemies' settlements!

DEFEAT CONDITION: You lose your starting point.

SITUATION: Both Septimus in the Southwest (S) and Ramadamses in the South (Ra) have extensive territory under their control. The threat of extinction weighs heavy on your commmand; the scarcity of food is a major concern.  There is hope. An island in the West (A) awaits with broad plains and uncultivated lands. You (TT) must find a way to expand into that uncultivated land.

Solution from:

 Ingo J. Timm

 

he game can be divided into three phases: 1. Building, 2. Defense, 3. Offense. The first phase will decide the success of the entire mission. Speed is of the essence here. The most important thing here is the rapid build-up of a defense. There is relatively little space. Your main task consists in establishing a complete infrastructure without an extensive expansion of territory to the south. You simply can’t afford to construct unnecessary buildings (mines are necessary). After tearing down all of your towers except those in the north, there will be enough room for the following buildings: a grain farm, a grain mill, an additional tower in the northeast (because the defeat condition is fulfilled if the small tower in the northeast is occupied), a bakery, a fisherman’s hut, a shipyard, a tool smith’s works, a weapon smith’s works, a barracks and a large residence.

The land in the west is a good place to build a landing dock after the land has been cleared. The following buildings have priority during the construction phase: tool smith’s works (hammers become scarce quickly), weapon smith’s works, barracks, fisherman’s hut and a large tower.

In the meantime, your geologists should find suitable locations for two coalmines, and one or two iron ore mines. These should be built quickly. Adjust your settings so that all coal and iron bars are brought to the weapon smith after one unit of coal and one iron bar has been delivered to the tool smith (all of the initial raw materials will be needed for producing bows). At the beginning, you can supply your coalmines as well as your iron ore mines with fish.

ou’ll need a transport ship quickly to pick up the 8 soldiers on the neighboring island. Your two large towers and eight bowmen (full garrisons on all towers) should be able to hold off the first wave of attacks.

In the second phase (defensive), you have to concentrate on getting control of the island in the north and the one in the south with your pioneers. Build a medium residence near the swamp. Then build a small tower on the land bridge to expand your territory, later a castle and two large towers.  Now it is time to recruit soldiers as quickly as possible (a lot of bowmen for the castle and two large towers). If your defense holds, you won’t have to worry about the Romans any longer. When the offensive phase begins, your supplies in the north will be sufficient for defense and you can build a large temple on the island in the west and a small temple and a rice farm on the island in the north.

Using divine intervention you can expand your army quickly. The island in the west can be added to your territory and there you can set up your food production. If your army is strong enough, you can take over the territory in the south by building towers. The Egyptians is the southeast are not as strong as the Romans – you can make use of the raw materials of the Egyptians and attack the Romans with increased strength. Be careful. The Romans are very strong!

 

Solution from:

 Marcel Jeske

 

ut down the trees on the main island to make room for construction and build sawmills. Expand to the south and you’ll find a lot of land just before your enemy’s border which is suitable for building farms.

If this is not enough land, it is also possible to build another settlement on the island to the west (this is not advisable because, although there are some presents there, building is difficult there on account of all the trees and a swamp. It is time-consuming and in the meantime, Septimus is growing in strength).

In the meantime you can bring the swordsmen from the small island to the main island. There should be enough gold, iron and coal to build up a small, but strong army quickly. The first attacks from Septimus will not take long. The large supplies of fish will be adequate for the miners until bread becomes available. Next, you should build two large temples and promote your swordsmen. You can do without the production of spears entirely and should cut down on the number of bows. Once you’ve withstood the enemy attacks with nearly no losses, you can first defeat Septimus and then Ramadamses.
