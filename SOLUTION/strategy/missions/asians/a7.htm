BRIDGEHEADS

VICTORY CONDITION: Conquer the large Egyptian guard tower (A) and the two castles (B) and (C)!

DEFEAT CONDITION: You have lost all of your buildings.

SITUATION: As long as the two crossings to your peninsula (TT) are controlled by the Romans (S) in the South and the Egyptians (Ra) in the West, your position is extremely perilous. Conquer both crossings.

Solution from:

 Marcel Jeske

 

his mission will become increasingly more difficult, the longer you leave your opponent in peace. Pause the game and tear down all towers except for the one in the south. Gather all of your soldiers in the south and north together. Next, build additional woodcutter’s huts and then tear down the tool smith’s works and the slaughterhouse (takes too long until meat is available). Send your geologist off in search of mineral resources and build another medium residence. Now you can continue the game (pause off). 

As soon as it becomes possible, build fisherman’s huts and mines. Gold should be produced and stored early on. Finally, you should build a waterworks, two weapon smith’s works, a barracks and a farm. Don’t forget to increase your settler population. Once your soldiers have a fighting strength of 50%, you can conquer the castle in the south with all your soldiers, leaving all of the soldiers involved in the attack behind as defense.

Septimus will try to reconquer the castle a couple times but will be too weak to be successful. As soon as you have enough soldiers available, the castle and large tower in the north should present no problems.

 

Solution from:

 Tobias

 

he buildings are far apart so you should solve your transport problems by building a large residence so that you always have enough carriers at hand. You will also need free settlers for recruiting soldiers. A small army is enough to capture the castles one after the other, ideally beginning with those in the south and then in the north.
