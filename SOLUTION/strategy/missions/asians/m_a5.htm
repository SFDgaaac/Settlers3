RICE WINE

VICTORY CONDITION: You must get all supplies of rice wine into your territory. So don't destroy the respective towers. Conquer them!  

DEFEAT CONDITION: Your colony has been completely destroyed.You destroyed one of the guard towers you should have conquered instead.

SITUATION: Planning your strategy is vital to success. Take your time building up your settlement before you attack the enemy. Cannons will serve you well here.A little advanced reconnaissance would be beneficial to your cause; find out where the enemy rice wine stores are kept. If you are able to capture those stores, you should be able to overtake the enemy colonies without having to throw the entire countryside into turmoil.

STORY:

wo of our trade ships carrying valuable shipments of rice wine have been seized by the Romans and Egyptians. The battle for supremacy in these new lands has put a severe strain on supplies and morale. Have the Gods gone mad in their thirst for fame, feast, and fortune? Is there such thirst in the Pantheon that the gods are battling each other for the sacrifices? 

We need to gain the advantage over our enemies quickly, or the only thing we'll have left to sacrifice to Ch'ih-Yu is ourselves.

 

Solution from:

 David Röthlisberger

 

elieve it or not: you do not have to fear any attacks in this entire mission!

1. Therefore, you have all the time in the world to expand your territory with towers, claim the entire island, and build up a large colony.

2. At first glance, the map designer appears to have been on your side: lots of space plus some sulfur in the north. Everything is right there, waiting for you... except for a swamp for growing rice! What now? With soldiers at the rank of private you wouldn’t stand a chance against your opponent: you would have to get something like 300 or 400 soldiers over to his side, and that would be far too much of an effort, right? Well, as you know, you gain eight points of manna for every large temple you build.

Since you have enough space, wood and stone, you should build approx. 20 to 30 large temples. Once the priest has left the temple, you can crush it: you will get the manna anyway. With this (admittedly unusual) strategy you are still going to be able to recruit soldiers at the rank of captain. 

3. In any case, you should keep mining gold until your fighting strength exceeds 100%.

4. Build a couple of cannons to be able to destroy some of your Egyptian opponent’s cannons in the north without having to leave your island.

5. Now you should keep on recruiting soldiers (300 or more).

6. Transport approx. 100 soldiers to the coasts of the Egyptians’ territory. Caution: do not attack any castles or towers! Once you have reached the liquor bottles you should capture the towers surrounding them. Now the bottles are yours!

7. Still, beating the Romans in the south is a lot more difficult. Make sure to land your troops just north of the liquor bottles. You will "just" find some small towers there. Caution: the Romans will dispatch an entire army immediately! Therefore, you should also send plenty of soldiers (200-300).

You should be able to get a foothold on the Romans’ island. Now, all you have to do is capture the couple of towers near the liquor bottles, and victory will be yours.
