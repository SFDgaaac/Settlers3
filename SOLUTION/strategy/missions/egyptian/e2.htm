THE LOOPHOLE

VICTORY CONDITION:
Conquer the entire island!

DEFEAT CONDITION:
You (Ra) or your allies (P) lose all your buildings.

SITUATION:
During your (Ra) victory celebrations Tsu Tang (TT) has overrun wide expanses of the island. It's going to be a long, hard battle taking it back. While seeking allies, you found a small Egyptian pirate colony in the Southeast (P). As long as Tsu Tang is positioned between you, there's no alternative but to try to keep him at bay until you and the allied pirates are strong enough to attack the Asians.

2. DIARY ENTRY OF RAMADAMSES:

verwhelmed by the success of our campaign against the pirates, I became foolhardy. And I didn't anticipate the appearance of the Chinese, led by Tsu Tang. They have forced my hand early, but I should've expected it. Tsu Tang knows we are the better administrators, and that he can only wield his military strength as long as our wealth and resources are weakened. But we are caught like a mouse in a hole, and the cat, Tsu Tang, need only wait for us to come out. We must try to find another way...and try to enlist allies in our cause.

 

 

Solution from:

 Marcel Jeske

 

rom the very beginning you should concentrate on getting building material. Then you can build a medium residence. For your expansion it is important that you first advance to the south a little and then eastward. (You can create some great farmland from the swamps if you like). Send about 6 pioneers to the west to reach some deposits of iron ore that are worthwhile. If you keep the gem miners well stocked with fish, you will have enough gems to defeat Tsu Tang.

If Tsu Tang attacks your ally who is often still quite weak, you can distract him by attacking one of his towers with a handful of soldiers. Normally, you won't have to promote your soldiers. If you have enough soldiers, victory is simply a matter of time.

 

Solution from:

 Uwe Linsenboll

 

his mission is actually quite simple. The best strategy is to expand first of all to take away as much room as possible from the Asians.

The allies in the lower area of the map will do the same. Okay: first, get your building industry going, then you can build up your infrastructure in peace, since the Asians are not very fast and don't get their industry going particularly quickly. There will be some attacks from the Asians now and then but they will be rather half-hearted and don't represent any big danger. Gather your troops together (the ones you've had from the very beginning) and only attack the Asians when they are in your territory. Build a healer's quarters to avoid casualties among your soldiers.

You can then begin mining gems from the southwestern part of the northernmost mountain. This will increase the fighting strength of your troops. By this time you should be getting additional soldiers. Don't bother mining gold. It doesn't help much. Gems are twice as effective.

I built a brewery, but promoting your troops really isn't necessary. As soon as you have about 40 soldiers, you'll be able to defeat the Asians in battle. (Actually, 30 are enough, but 40 is safer. It depends on your skills in battle).
