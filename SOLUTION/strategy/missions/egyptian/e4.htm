THE ROMANS

VICTORY CONDITION:
Destroy Septimus Marius' settlement!

DEFEAT CONDITION:
You lose your southern outpost.

SITUATION:
The Romans in the south (S) are very strong, and you (Ra) could lose all hope of advancing here--if Septimus Marius weren't having problems of his own with pirates (P), colonizing to his West. That will at least rob him of some precious time.

4. DIARY ENTRY OF RAMADAMSES:

su Tang has expanded too quickly and now faces serious economic difficulty, as he should have expected. Our drive to the West may not be making much progress, but it may just weaken Tsu Tang enough for our movement in the South to meet with success. We must not hesitate!

The southern islands are stable and prosperous, but there we confront a new enemy: the Romans under Septimus Marius. He has expanded his colony so far to the North that we will come into conflict with him soon. They are strong. We must hurry.

 

Solution from:

 Marcel Jeske

 

his mission is easier than it appears. First, buildings have to be constructed on all three islands so that a supply of building materials is ensured.

 

The island to the far east has deposits of gems. The mining and storage of the gems takes place there while iron and coal are being mined on the island in the middle. Fishermen are sufficient for supplying your miners with food. All your soldiers can be transported to the island of your opponent using transport ships. In order to maximize the number of soldiers at your disposal, tear down all towers except one. If that is not enough, you can recruit more soldiers.

If your soldiers have a minimum of 67% fighting strength, there should be no difficulties defeating Septimus. If enough of your soldiers are still healthy, you can also destroy the pirates right afterwards. It is important to keep your soldiers together and not let them scatter out. Speed is your ally in this mission because Septimus has poor prospects when you attack early and with strength.
