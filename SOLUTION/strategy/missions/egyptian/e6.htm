THE NORTHERN FLANK

VICTORY CONDITION:
Destroy Septimus Marius' settlement in the Southwest!

DEFEAT CONDITION:
You lose all your buildings.

SITUATION:
Tsu Tang (TT) remains firmly planted, as if nothing were happening in the northwest. He has entered into another alliance with Septimus (S). These two foes threaten to thwart your advance to the west once and for all.

6. DIARY ENTRY OF RAMADAMSES:

ur position has become more secure, but we are still pressed between Septimus and Tsu Tang. Septimus took advantage of our short alliance by thrusting deep into the west, but to keep his northern flank free - and because my forces have become too much of a threat - he threw in his lot with Tsu Tang once more. They plan to crush us, and then turn on each other to determine a victor.

Now we must break out and destroy the trades routes in the west, and we must attempt to drive a deeper wedge into the south. Against Tsu Tang I can only remain on the defensive. But the longer I can hinder his progress, the more his economic deficits bear down upon him....

 

 

Solution from:

 Marcel Jeske

 

his mission can be very difficult in some cases. Septimus is very strong at the beginning and is able to attack with a horde of troops in the first minutes of the game. Right at the very beginning, Septimus has to be weakened. Tear down all of your towers and gather your soldiers together. (Leave the castle standing!). While you are building up your infrastructure, send all your soldiers to the west and attack the first enemy tower. The following tactic has proved itself:

  1. Attack the tower and tear it down immediately after it has been captured.

  2. Your opponent will send soldiers to the place where the tower stood. These soldiers will only defend themselves when they have gotten there. Therefore, you have to do everything possible to ensure they don't get there.

  3. Repeat this tactic with every enemy tower.

During your attacks, always move westward splitting the land into two parts. Destroy the temples and the industry surrounding the first mountain. After you've managed this, you'll have about 10 soldiers remaining. Now it is time to retreat. Have your healer restore your soldiers to full health and man your castle completely. If Tsu-Tang launches an attack (this seldom happens), he has to attack the castle, but he'll fail miserably. After you've recruited more soldiers, you can deal Septimus the death blow.
