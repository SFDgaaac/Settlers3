THREE ISLANDS

VICTORY CONDITION:
Colonize the islands A,B, and C completely, and eliminate your enemies.

DEFEAT CONDITION:
Tsu Tang colonizes the entire island A.

SITUATION:
The land is very mountainous, and it will be difficult growing crops on your rocky islands (Ra1, Ra2). Initially, gathering resources will be equally as difficult. Two small, fertile islands in the Northwest (B, C) could help you with your food production problems. A large, mineral rich island to the West (A) should help you with building up your resources. Be mindful of the Asians, Tsu Tang (TT) has established an outpost nearby. It must be destroyed.

7. DIARY ENTRY OF RAMADAMSES:

ur wedge into the west is almost complete. There are only three islands left for us to secure and settle, but one of them is of good size. Then we will resolve things once and for all...and Horus will be pleased.

 

 

Solution from:

 Marcel Jeske

 

ll you need to win here is a stonecutter, two woodcutters and then a sawmill. Pioneers can expand your territory over the rest of the island. On the small island in the north, you can build a gem mine on the small hill. Tear down all of your towers on the main island so that you have more space.

With a coal mine and iron mine, an iron smelting works and a shipyard, you'll be able to transport soldiers from both islands to the big island. Your miners can get along quite well with fish. The island can be quickly taken with some 40 pioneers. Your opponent often blocks his own way so that he cannot expand further. If not, you simply have to proceed quickly. You'll find weapons, building materials and gold as you go along.

Build a barracks on the new island and have weapons delivered from your main island. You now have to gather enough soldiers together with a fighting strength of 57% (if you prefer more fighting strength, you'll have to see to getting more gold or gems) in order to rid the island of your opponent. Finally, towers have to be built on the two small islands.

 

Solution from:

 Martin Biastoch

 

rect the following buildings on the island (Ra1): a stonecutter's hut, awoodcutter's hut and a sawmill. On the other island (Ra2) build a gem mine in the middle of the mountain. Back on the other island (Ra1), have a geologist explore the mountain in the middle of the island. After all the construction is complete on the island (Ra1), you can tear down the tower to the west. Build a shipyard a little to the south and then you'll need a landing dock, an iron mine, a coal mine and an iron smelting works. If trees become scarce, be sure to build a forester's hut. The landing dock building should be built where the tower previously was. Use two pioneers to gain more space to the north. If you also expand a bit toward the south, you'll find a couple gold bars.

Once the shipyard is finished, build a transport ship and then a trade ship. Use the transport ship to take the carriers from on the island (Ra2) to the island (A) after converting them to pioneers. Begin taking island (A) from the north because you'll find weapons and gold there.

Now you're ready to transport all the soldiers from the island to island (A). When all your soldiers are on the island and you have the gold in your possession, begin capturing as many towers as necessary from your opponent who has settled in the south of island (A) until there are no more enemy mines in operation. By building a barracks and a medium residence on island (A), enough new soldiers will become available so that you can conquer the rest of the island. Islands (B) and (C) can then be taken with pioneers. All the tools you'll need for building there can be transported to the island with your trade ship.
