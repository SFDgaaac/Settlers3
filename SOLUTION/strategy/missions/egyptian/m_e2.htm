BROTHERS IN ARMS

VICTORY CONDITION:
Build a pyramid, a sphinx and a large residential building.Destroy the Romans.

DEFEAT CONDITION:
Your colony or your ally has been completely destroyed.

SITUATION:
The Egyptians are located northeast of you, and are protected by a land bridge which separates you from the Romans. Because stone is scarce in your area, your ally will ship you stone by caravan after a while. In return you should provide them with iron and/or weapons, so that they can defend themselves.

If possible, you should try to keep the Romans occupied with attacks from the sea to hinder their invasion of your ally. A strategically clever pinpoint attack could be very effective, but only with sufficient manpower and a little divine assistance.

STORY:

e have left paradise behind us, and carried on with our search for our homeland. Along the way, we found another island, where a group of our countrymen was colonizing. Stranded themselves, they could offer us no new information about our whereabouts. They are under heavy pressure from a Roman colony in the north.

They have very little iron and have asked us for help. They offered, in return, to help us with gathering stone.

Our thief's reports confirm that only together will we stand a chance against the Romans, if we have a chance at all, that is. We are greatly outnumbered, and will surely need help from our god, Horus.

 

 

Solution from:

 David Röthlisberger

 

he supplies of stone that your ally delivers would be enough for two settlements! For the most part, the Romans will leave your ally in peace; they will be concentrating their attention on you. Your first priority is to protect yourself. You should only deliver goods to your ally when you are in a position to do this safely.

1. Thanks to the large supply of stone from your ally, you can build up a large colony quickly. Build several grain farms so that you can promote your soldiers quickly.

2. The two mountains offer all the mineral resources you need. Produce as many gold bars as you can.

3. Once your soldiers are promoted, crank up your recruiting. Supply your ally with some iron ore so that he can arm his troops better.

4. 200 soldiers should be enough to defeat the Romans in most cases.

5. If you have already build a large residence, a sphinx and pyramid in the meantime, the defeat of the Romans means you've won the mission.
