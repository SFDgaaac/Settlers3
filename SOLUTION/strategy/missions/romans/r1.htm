FIRST STEPS

VICTORY CONDITION:   Conquer the entire island!

DEFEAT CONDITION:  Your settlement is destroyed.

SITUATION: A rebellion (R) has sprung in the Northwest. A small band of my countrymen have established a foothold there. Unfortunately, they control the only gold resources (S) on the island. If this rebel colony goes unchallenged, they may become too powerful to contend with.

1. DIARY ENTRY OF SEPTIMUS MARIUS:

don't know what happened. Yesterday I was captain of a trader plying the seas, and that's all I ever wanted to be. I was free - I saw distant lands others only heard tale of; I was happy.

Now, thanks to Jupiter, I am a ruler bound to duty in a foreign world. That it is unknown is of no consequence; the unknown I am accustomed to. But I am expected to rule here, to wage war and to win. For Jupiter, greater glory. For me, only a test...and at the end only freedom or ruin, like every voyage upon the sea.

I do not know if I will be an able commander, but hope will be my anchor. If Hannibal - nightmare of ancient Rome and paragon of strategists - can spring upon the world from a family of traders, so can I. Indeed, my empire this day is no greater than many a ship that sailed under my command.

Solution from:

asically, you can win this level with the army you have right at the beginning of the game…

All you have to do is gather your troops, move them up north and vanquish the enemy. However, there is not much to learn by doing so, right? So take your time: start by slowly building up your timber industry (one forester, two woodcutters and one sawmill), then have a look around for some stone, expand your settlement, and find some more resources. Go on by building up your food production, and then build some mines.

Iron smelting works + weapon smith + barracks = new soldiers.
Vineyard + temple = possible promotion for soldiers
Gold smelting works + storage area = increase soldiers’ motivation

Just try out everything to get a feeling for the game!

Make sure that you always have enough Settlers! (Hint: Build residences!)

 
Solution from: Marc Bethke
 

asically, it's possible to 'rush' this map right at the beginning by launching an immediate attack on your opponent with all of your soldiers. To do this, simply gather your soldiers together, send them up to the northwest, and let them attack first the one enemy tower, then the other. Since your opponent is still very weak at this point in the game, he cannot defend himself against your attack, and will lose the mission. However, as this strategy of immediate attack ('rushing') will no longer work later in the campaign, you should use this mission to get acquainted with the game instead. Since you are never really in danger of losing the game in this mission, you are free to experiment a little bit.

First, you should build up a proper building and construction industry. Build a forester's hut (next to a forest, of course), two woodcutter's huts (build one forester's hut for every two woodcutter's huts), a sawmill (next to the woodcutter's huts), and a stonecutter's hut (next to some stones, of course).   You should also build a medium residence (small residences are too ineffective!) to make sure you have enough Settlers. Actually, you should keep an eye on your number of Settlers during the entire game, and build some more residences from time to time (later in the game, you may also build large residences, for they are even more effective than the medium ones). Furthermore, you should always make sure you have enough space for expanding your colony. Basically, there are two ways of expanding your territory.

The first one is to build towers. The disadvantage here is wasting valuable building materials for the tower's construction, and ordering off a soldier to occupy the tower. Of course, you can crush the tower immediately after the soldier has moved in (the land will stay yours), but keep in mind that you always must have at least one tower; otherwise, you will lose the game instantly.

he second way to expand your territory is with pioneers. Of course, you must have some unoccupied Settlers before you can recruit pioneers. Send your pioneers to your borders and let them claim new territory. With both of these ways of expanding your territory, you should keep in mind that your opponent may also quite easily claim any land (with pioneers or by building towers or castles), which is not in the vicinity of a tower or castle.

After getting your building and construction industry going, you should focus on building up your food production and heavy industry (the latter next to a mountain). You should also start growing wine to be able to upgrade your soldiers' fighting strength later on. Build the wineries in the vicinity of hills or at the foot of green mountains, and, for best results, select the working area to be either on a hill or the green part of a mountain (never build anything on those wine growing areas!) Don't forget to build a couple of small temples, where you can get manna points by making offerings of wine (keep your transport routes short!). Once your heavy industry is up and running (incl. the production of gold and weapons), you should build a barracks for recruiting soldiers. Please note that you'll need some unoccupied Settlers for this, too. If you think you're finished playing around, let your troops attack your opponent to win the mission.
