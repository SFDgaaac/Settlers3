NORTHERN MEETINGS

VICTORY CONDITION: Destroy all settlements of Remigius (Re) and Ramadamses (Ra)!

DEFEAT CONDITION: You lose your northeastern outpost (S2).

SITUATION: Hard on the heels of Remigius, you have made your way north (S1) and are now confronted with a new dilemma. A well-secured pirate island (P), as well as Remigius' weakened colony (Re) stands united; apparently he has allied with a nearby Egyptian settlement, under the rule of someone called Ramadamses (Ra). Your outpost (S2)will need to be well fortified, if you expect to survive this enemy. In order to demonstrate your peaceful intentions to the pirates, you have declared the desert island (W) nearby as neutral territory.

Solution from:

 Marcel Jeske

 

onquer the entire main island and increase your production of food and mining of resources. Crush coalmines that are no longer productive. Keep an eye on the production of gold and wine.

Transport all swordsmen to your outpost, which should be able to support itself after some time. After making sure that you have sufficient building materials, build a fisherman’s hut, a coalmine and an iron mine. Once you have a tool smith’s works on your outpost island you can consider it independent from the main island. Once your offensive strength has reached 60% or more (soldiers should have been promoted at least once), and you also have enough soldiers, you should strike against Ramadamses (green). Afterwards, you should us your transport ships to transport your remaining soldiers to the yellow opponent’s territory. In most cases, he should be very easy to beat.

With the release of version 1.23 the mission briefing is correct: you don’t have to attack the pirates to win the map.

 

Solution from:

 jsd98

 

nce again you start on two islands: a main island and an outpost. Therefore, the first basic steps should be quite clear:

build a medium residence on your main island
turn some of your Settlers into pioneers to conquer the surrounding land
build up a complete economy (incl. mines, iron smelting works, tool smith’s hut, temple)

It is crucial to build a shipyard early to be able to build a transport ship and, later on, a trade ship and some more transport ships. You also have to establish a trading route between your two islands. Just like on your main island, you should build a medium residence on your outpost island, and then turn some of the Settlers into pioneers to conquer the island quickly. Again you should expand your territory on the outpost island only until you reach the land bridge, and secure your territorial gains with a guard tower. Once you have established a trading route between the two islands, start transporting goods to your outpost. However, it is even more important to get some soldiers over to your outpost! Feel free to go on expanding your territory once you feel safe enough. Still, it is NOT necessary to expand your territory…

I’ve continued building transport ships, packing them with soldiers, sending them to the outpost, and eventually got rid of Ramadamses. With the rest of my soldiers I landed on the small island in the northwest and vanquished my opponent there. If the game is still going on you also have to beat the pirates. It’s a good idea to start the attack on the pirates by sending some soldiers across the land bridge. Once the battle is in full swing you should land about ten transport ships full of soldiers on the other side of the pirates’ territory, thereby encircling them.

 

Solution from:

 Tobias

 

ou will have to have a thriving economy on your main island to produce soldiers quickly enough.

You will have to have a thriving economy on your main island to produce soldiers quickly enough.

You won’t find much stone on your main island. Therefore, you should build two stonecutter’s huts on the outpost island, and transport the stone over to the main island. To supply your gold mines on the main island with fish you can also import it from the outpost island. However, you should make sure that there are enough stones left on the outpost island, while at the same time enough wood is imported from the main island. Otherwise, you won’t have anything to erect buildings with!

Once your offensive strength is between 70% and 80% you have a good chance of defeating your opponent in the northeast with 20 or 30 soldiers. First you should cut his territory in half to weaken his economy before dealing with the two parts one after the other. Make sure you always have enough swordsmen to occupy the towers you conquer. When all else fails, you can crush recently conquered towers to free up more swordsmen.

To vanquish the other two opponents send seven transport ships full of soldiers to the two islands up north. Conquer the northernmost island first, and then deal with the pirates.
