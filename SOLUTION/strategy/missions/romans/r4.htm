TROUBLE ON THE WESTERN FRONT

VICTORY CONDITION: Conquer the entire island!

DEFEAT CONDITION: You (S) lose all your buildings.

SITUATION: Remigius has been defeated and you've advanced further to the West (S). Ramadamses' colony in the North (Ra) is well established. He appears to have developed an alliance with the traitor pirates in the Northwest (P). You'll have to act quickly in order to survive.

Solution from:

 Marcel Jeske

 

t the beginning you should focus on expanding your territory and producing building materials (stone). Ideally, your territory should stretch from the western mountains to the eastern ones. You should expand your territory to the north until you reach the bay where a lot of stones are waiting for you.

Once you have three stonecutter’s huts, four woodcutter’s huts, two sawmills and one forester, it should be easy to acquire gold and weapons quickly. If you are not satisfied with the quantity of stone you find at the northern bay, there is still more to be found in the south.

As long as your castle is still standing, you may safely crush all of your towers. By doing so you not only gain valuable building materials, but you also force your opponent to concentrate his attacks on the castle. However, if your opponent’s territory is getting too close to your own, you should quickly build some towers to avoid territorial losses.

There is plenty of room for farms, but, considering the time it takes the grain to grow, you should build grain farms at an early stage. You should take advantage of the fact that just west of your castle the sea is full of fish. Use land you can spare for wineries to increase your offensive strength.

You can easily repel smaller attacks. The opponent who strikes first against you is sure to be the first one to be destroyed. (The blue opponent has often piled up quite a lot of gold, which makes attacking the yellow opponent later on easier.)

 

Solution from:

 Tobias

 

layers start in the southeast and should keep an eye on the northern border separating them from the opponent in the east. That is going to be attacked quite frequently at the beginning. For that reason you should place all of your troops near that border. It is a good idea to build a castle near that border to make your opponent think twice about attacking you there.

Once your economy is up and running, and you have produced a sufficient number of (strong!) soldiers you may risk attacking your opponent in the north (your eastern opponent). As soon as you have vanquished him you can start attacking the pirates, which should be easier, now that you can attack from two sides (south and east). Quite soon, there shouldn’t be any trace left of those guys...

 

Solution from:

 eYe

 

irst of all, there are two things to keep in mind: the map shown in the briefing is not correct (the starting positions S and P swapped places), and you better forget about those tips you've read in the briefing or in games magazines. This mission is very hard, especially for beginners who try to quickly expand their territory to the north and northwest to build up a "normal" economy: they are bound to be attacked by both opponents and will lose the game in no time.

I suggest you do NOT expand your territory, especially NOT in your opponent's direction. That way you will gain valuable time to prepare for the first couple of attacks your opponent will launch against you.

Try this: build two woodcutter's huts, then two fisherman's huts, a storage area and a medium residence. Send a few geologists to the mountains in the east. Crush the castle, for it takes up precious space, but don't touch your towers. Now you have some time to make the following menu adjustments.

n the 'Transportation Priorities' menu select fish to be stored, and in the menu 'Distribution of Goods' set fish to approx. 2/3 coalmines and 1/3 iron mines (for the production of weapons you will need exactly twice as much coal as iron). Finally, set 20 bows in the 'Tools and Weapons' menu at the highest priority (of course, you also have to recruit 20 bowmen!). To be able to actually produce those weapons you have to build a coal and an iron mine (your geologists should have found something by now!). Now you should start building an iron smelting works, a weapon smith's works and barracks. To avoid running out of building materials you build two woodcutter's huts, a sawmill and a stonecutter's hut. Soon the first couple of bowmen will gather in front of your barracks. Now you can start erecting a defense perimeter consisting of guard towers from the north to the northwest, which have to be pretty close to one another. Your 20 bowmen are enough to man ten towers (plus a swordsman for each tower, of course). You should also gather the rest of your soldiers behind those towers. Now your opponent is more than welcome to try his luck at breaking through: you are well prepared!

fter you have survived the first attack waves (with a little bit of luck your bowmen will strike so much fear into the hearts of your opponent's troops that they won't even dare attack!) you should simply go on building up and strengthening your economy. North of your defense perimeter there is enough space for two or three grain farms. Build up a complete food production cycle now. Expand your territory by sending pioneers to the west and east. Keep on building more mines and start producing gold and manna. Make sure to send all the fish to the gold mines ('Distribution of Goods' menu). Increase your production of soldiers by building a tool smith's works (to get more hammers), more iron smelting works and weapon smith's works.

Once you have enough well trained soldiers and your storage areas are filled with gold, you can launch an attack on Ramadamses (yellow). It's a good idea to start the attack from the east and destroy the Egyptians' barracks. After your victory over your yellow opponent you can attack your blue opponent from several directions. He should not stand a chance, and your troops should be able to finish him off in no time.
