THE RACE

VICTORY CONDITION: Conquer the three islands to the North!

DEFEAT CONDITION:One of your opponents (A1, A2, A3) is able to amass more than 70 gold bullion.

SITUATION: A new foe has been found. Three Asian colonies (A1, A2, A3) have been spotted to the North, each struggling for control over the other. None of these tribes appear to be under the rule of the ruthless warrior Tsu Tang (TT), who I hear is gaining power.

Solution from:

 Marcel Jeske

 

f one of your opponents accumulates more than 70 gold bars, you've lost the game. For that reason you should crush your castle, the large tower and all guard towers but one. Now you should have enough building materials to start producing your own gold soon.

Conquer the entire island and chop down the small forest in the south. Start producing weapons and tools, and build a large temple. As soon as the large temple is finished, you can speed up the grain's growth once. After that, the large temple is useless and should be crushed. Two weapon smith's works should be enough, make sure to build three transport ships. Attack the opponent with the biggest gold reserves first. Since every one of your opponents has a large number of soldiers, your troops must have a fighting strength of at least 70% to stand a chance. Concentrate all available troops outside the opponent's territory, and then begin your attack. (Most of the time your orange opponent has quite a lot of gold. After you have dealt with him, strike against your yellow and green opponents).

 

Solution from:

 Tobias

 

n this mission you have to defeat the Asians (red) in the north. There is hardly any space on your main island, but it is enough to build the essential buildings. The land bridge in the south is an ideal location for building farms. To save space on the main island you should store your gold reserves on the small, uninhabited island in the northwest. You should also recruit your soldiers on that island to prevent all of your carriers on the main island from being turned into soldiers (there is plenty of room for new residences on the small island). Since the fighting strength of your opponent's troops is very high, the key to your victory lies in outnumbering those troops.
